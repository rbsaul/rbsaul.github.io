I am currently a Machine Learning Researcher at Booz Allen Hamilton, where I work under Dr. Edward Raff on deep learning approaches to cybersecurity problems (e.g. malware detection and analysis). In addition to machine learning, I have also worked in cryptography. I am broadly interested in machine learning that serves the public good and national interest.

I received my B.A. from Harvard in Spring 2022, graduating with highest honors in Mathematics, a minor in Computer Science, and a language citation in Arabic. I wrote my senior thesis on efficient factoring and the number field sieve, advised by Professor Noam Elkies. My talk on "Factoring and the Quadratic Sieve", based on the second chapter of my thesis, won the 2022 Robert Fletcher Rogers Award for the best undergraduate math talk. In addition to my academic pursuits, I worked as a peer tutor for the Harvard College Writing Center (three years) and played goalie for the Harvard Women's Club Soccer team (four years).

I am committed to increasing the participation of under-represented groups in computer science. Outside of the office, I volunteer with A-OK Mentoring-Tutoring, a small nonprofit that works with local elementary and middle school students in need of extra support.

I attended NeurIPS 2022, where I presented my paper on Lempel-Ziv Networks at the I Can't Believe It's Not Better workshop on empirical falsification.
